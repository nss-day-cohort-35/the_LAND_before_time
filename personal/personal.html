The Struggling Fame

Growing up Sober

Born in London, England, on September 14, 1983. Amy started messign around with music at a young age, playing on her brothers guitar. When she turned 14 she bought her own and started playing music. She starting chasing music a year later. She started with a group called the 'Bolsha Band', but worked as an entertainment journalist for World Entertainment News Network, in addition to singing for her group. Then in July 2000 she became a featured vocalist for the National Youth Jazz Orchestra. Amys bestfirend, soul signer Tyler James then sent a demo tape Amys to some talent scouts. Darcus Beese, a talent scout for Island, heard of Amy by accident when the manager of Lewinson Brothers showed him some of Amy's work. Although he wasnt able to find out who she was from Beese, and it took several months of searching around to find out who this extrodinary talent was.

Spark of it all

On October 20th, 2003, Amy's debut album Frank was released. Produced mainly by Salaam Remi, many songs were influenced by jazzy type music and , apart from two songs, Winehouse co-wrote every song. Then in 2004 her album hit upper charts, and got nominated for the Britsh Awards for "Britsh Female Solo Artist" and "Britsh Urban Act." Frank would eventually hit platnium sales. In May 2006, Winehouse's demo tracks such as "You Know I'm No Good" and "Rehab" appeared on Mark Ronson's New York radio show on East Village Radio. They were some of her first songs to appear on the radio after the release of "Pumps" and both expected to be on her next album. Promotion of Back to Black soon began and, in early October 2006 Winehouse's official website was relaunched with a new layout and clips of previously unreleased songs. "Back to Black" was released in the UK on 30 October 2006. It went to number one on the UK Albums Chart for two weeks in January 2007, dropping then climbing back for several weeks in February. In the US, it entered at number seven on the Billboard 200. It was the best-selling album in the UK of 2007, selling 1.85 million copies over the course of the year. The album had multiple hit songs such as "Rehab" and went #1 on the UK billboards in early 2007.

Love for Love, or Love for Use?

Its no secret that Amy has struggled with drug and alcohol addiction. Her husband, Blake Feilder-Civil, said himself that he is the reason she started doing drugs and drinking heavily. Like most A-list celeberties battling addiction, it was no easy task to stop. She did try and go to rehab and had gotten sober for months before her first relapes. They would end up getting a divorce with the help of Amy's fathe, who ended up forging her signiture on the divorce papers, as he and blake decided it was in the best intrest of her rehab to seperate. Amy would later go on to say she went months before even knowing they were divorced. Alochol was what in the long run ended things for this superstar. On July 23rd 2011, Amy was found dead in her house due to an apperant accidental overdose. Her B.A.C (Blood Alcohol Content) was at a .416 and she had two largre empty vodka bottles in her room.
